📘 What are Cyber Attacks?

Cyber attacks are malicious attempts to access, damage, or disrupt systems, networks, or data. Attackers exploit vulnerabilities in software, human behavior, or network configurations to gain unauthorized access.

⚠️ Major Types of Cyber Attacks

  • Phishing: Fake emails/websites used to steal sensitive data
  • Malware: Malicious software like viruses and trojans
  • Ransomware: Locks your data and demands payment
  • DDoS: Overloads systems to make them unavailable

🌍 Real-World Case Study

In the Colonial Pipeline attack (2021), ransomware forced the shutdown of a major fuel pipeline in the U.S., causing nationwide panic and fuel shortages. The company paid millions in ransom.

🎯 Phishing Deep Dive

Phishing attacks trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords or credit card details. These attacks often appear as legitimate emails from trusted organizations.

  • Fake login pages
  • Email spoofing
  • Urgent action messages

🦠 Malware Breakdown

Malware is designed to damage or exploit systems. It includes viruses, worms, trojans, spyware, and ransomware.

  • Keyloggers steal passwords
  • Spyware tracks activity
  • Ransomware encrypts files

🛠 Practical Security Tips

  • Never click unknown email links
  • Verify URLs before login
  • Use antivirus software
  • Keep systems updated

⚙️ Tools Used to Analyze Attacks

  • Wireshark (network traffic analysis)
  • Burp Suite (web security testing)
  • VirusTotal (file scanning)
